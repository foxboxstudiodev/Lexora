# Lexora Master Plan

Status: ACTIVE.

This is the new main planning document for Lexora.

## 1. Platform Scope

Lexora is not Android-only.

Lexora is planned for:

1. Android — Google Play Market.
2. iOS — Apple App Store.
3. Web/PWA — supporting channel.

Main release target:

```text
Android + iOS
Google Play Market + Apple App Store
```

Android and iOS must be planned together as first-class mobile release paths.

Android documents cover Android implementation, Google Play packaging, AAB builds, Android screenshots, Android icons, Android testing, and Google Play requirements.

iOS documents must be added next to Android documents and must cover Apple App Store release, iOS builds, iPhone/iPad screenshots where required, iOS icons, signing, TestFlight, App Store review, App Privacy, and Apple policy requirements.

Web/PWA remains a supporting channel, not the primary commercial target.

## 2. Level Structure

Lexora uses 1000 levels per language.

Each language has exactly 20 blocks of 50 levels:

```text
Block 1: 1-50
Block 2: 51-100
Block 3: 101-150
...
Block 20: 951-1000
```

Every 50-level block repeats the same internal difficulty law:

```text
1-10   easy
11-25  medium
26-40  hard
41-50  very hard / final
```

Each new block starts easier relative to that block, but the global difficulty still rises from Block 1 to Block 20.

## 3. Thematic Block Journey

Lexora uses 20 thematic blocks to make progression feel like a journey, not a flat list of levels.

The themes must move from simple and familiar worlds into adventure, history, magic, science fiction, and a final Lexora mythology.

Final block order:

```text
1. Sunny Valley
2. Forest Secrets
3. Sea Coast
4. City of Words
5. Mountain Peaks
6. Desert of Treasures
7. Jungle Adventure
8. Northern Lights
9. Ancient Egypt
10. Ancient World
11. Eastern Bazaar
12. Samurai Japan
13. Medieval Kingdom
14. World of Magic
15. Underwater Civilization
16. Sky Islands
17. Steampunk Metropolis
18. Space Expedition
19. Parallel Worlds
20. Temple of Lexora
```

Professional pacing logic:

```text
Blocks 1-5: soft start — nature, city, mountains.
Blocks 6-10: adventure and ancient civilizations.
Blocks 11-15: culture, legends, magic, ocean mystery.
Blocks 16-20: fantasy escalation, science fiction, cosmic scale, final Lexora myth.
```

Themes define the visual atmosphere, backgrounds, map progression, rewards, chapter identity, and player emotion.

Level words do not have to be strictly limited to the block theme. The word database must prioritize real, natural, high-quality nouns. Theme restriction must never damage word quality.

## 4. Four-Layer Structure Inside Every Block

Every 50-level thematic block must contain 4 internal visual and emotional layers matching the difficulty law:

```text
1-10   easy layer
11-25  medium layer
26-40  hard layer
41-50  very hard / final layer
```

These layers make each block feel like a small journey with a beginning, development, challenge, and finale.

### 1. Sunny Valley

1-10 — Morning Garden
Sun, grass, tree, house, birds. The player learns the game.

Houses, fences, well, road, farm. The player is already engaged.
