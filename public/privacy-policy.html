Lexora Privacy Policy

Lexora is a multilingual word-connect crossword puzzle game. This Privacy Policy explains how Lexora handles information when you use the game.

Information we collect

Lexora does not require an account and does not ask you to provide your name, email address, phone number, contacts, photos, precise location, or payment information inside the game.

The game stores gameplay progress locally on your device or browser. This can include selected language, completed levels, coins, settings, daily reward state, achievements, and gameplay statistics.

Local storage

Lexora uses local storage to keep your progress and settings on the same device. This information is used only to make the game work, restore progress, and remember preferences.

Analytics, advertising, and tracking

The current Lexora build does not include advertising SDKs, third-party analytics SDKs, Firebase Analytics, Google Analytics, AdMob, or cross-app tracking code.

Children

Lexora is a general puzzle game. It is not designed to collect personal information from children. If a parent or guardian believes that personal information has been provided, they can contact us to request deletion.

Data sharing

Lexora does not sell personal information. The game does not transmit local gameplay progress to a Lexora server in the current build.

Service providers

The web version is hosted through Vercel. Hosting providers may process technical information such as IP address and request logs as part of normal website delivery and security. Their handling of such data is governed by their own policies.

Data deletion

You can clear locally stored game progress by clearing the app or browser storage for Lexora on your device. Uninstalling the app may also remove local game data, depending on platform behavior.

Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy when Lexora changes. The effective date above will be updated when material changes are made.
